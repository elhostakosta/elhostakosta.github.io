About Me

Hi, I’m Abdelrahman Mahmoud, but you might know me better as elhostakosta, the username I use everywhere. I’m a recent Computer Science graduate from Helwan University in Egypt, with a lifelong passion for problem-solving and understanding how things work. Ever since I was young, I’ve been fascinated by subjects like physics and math, and I’ve always enjoyed taking things apart to see their inner workings. That curiosity has now evolved into a love for building useful, impactful projects and collaborating with others to create something awesome. What excites me most about coding is the ability to bring ideas to life, solving real-world problems and creating something tangible out of thin air. I’m language-agnostic, focusing more on the problem or project at hand and choosing the best tools and technologies to get the job done.

My journey into coding began by accident during middle school. Thanks to my academic achievements, my school enrolled me in a government-supported program that taught the basics of competitive programming using C++. The program, led by university students, aimed to prepare us for the Egyptian Olympiad in Informatics. While I didn’t excel in the program or qualify for the competition, it planted the seed of curiosity. At the time, I didn’t have internet access, so I focused on school and forgot about programming until university, where I majored in Computer Science. My “aha” moment came when I realized I could actually solve problems and build projects. I learned that with time, planning, and persistence, I could find solutions to almost anything. Reflecting on my early setback, I adopted a growth mindset: the more I practiced, the better I became. What excites me most now is working on projects that have the potential to change lives and make a real impact.
